Calton Hill

Calton Hill is a hill in central Edinburgh, Scotland, situated beyond the east end of Princes Street and included in the city's UNESCO World Heritage Site. Views of, and from, the hill are often used in photographs and paintings of the city.

The hill is the location of several iconic monuments and buildings, including the National Monument, the Nelson Monument, the Dugald Stewart Monument, and the City Observatory. It is a popular spot for both locals and tourists to visit due to its panoramic views of the city skyline and surrounding areas.

Calton Hill is also known for its role in the city's annual Hogmanay celebrations, with the hill providing a vantage point for watching the fireworks and festivities.
