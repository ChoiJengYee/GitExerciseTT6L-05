Byōdō-in Temple

The Byōdō-in Temple is a Buddhist temple in the city of Uji in Kyoto Prefecture, Japan. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is famous for its Phoenix Hall, a national treasure of Japan and the iconic image on the back of the 10 yen coin.

The temple was originally built in 998 during the Heian period as a rural villa for the powerful Fujiwara clan. It was later converted into a temple by Fujiwara no Yorimichi in 1052. The Phoenix Hall, completed in 1053, is the main building of the temple and is known for its graceful architecture and beautiful setting surrounded by a large pond.

The Byōdō-in Temple is considered a masterpiece of Buddhist art and architecture and is a popular tourist destination in Japan.
